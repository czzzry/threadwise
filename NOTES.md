# Teaching Notes

- The founder learns best from zoomed-out explanations that connect code decisions to product strategy.
- Diagrams, mental models, and mnemonics are high value.
- The founder wants to understand how the pieces fit together well enough to speak truthfully and make future decisions, not just memorize function names.
- Keep the teaching grounded in the actual Threadwise implementation and tradeoffs.
- For analytics teaching, emphasize how the founder will operate the product and make decisions. Do not belabor privacy; the founder is comfortable enabling live inbox analytics. Explain omitted raw email text primarily as analytically useless high-cardinality data, not as moralizing.
