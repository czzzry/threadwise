# Teaching Notes

- The founder learns best from zoomed-out explanations that connect code decisions to product strategy.
- Diagrams, mental models, and mnemonics are high value.
- The founder wants to understand how the pieces fit together well enough to speak truthfully and make future decisions, not just memorize function names.
- Keep the teaching grounded in the actual Threadwise implementation and tradeoffs.
- Always explain meaningful implementation work in architectural terms: component boundaries, data flow, state, authority, failure behavior, privacy, and tradeoffs—not only what a feature does.
- For analytics, distinguish product behavior, observability, automated tests, and classifier evaluation; the founder wants to know precisely how services connect and what data crosses each boundary.
- Lesson HTML is a durable preparation and reference artifact, not the assessment. Do not make the founder self-grade from an answer key by default.
- Run retrieval, evaluation, targeted explanation, retesting, and learning-record updates in the Codex conversation so the founder can answer by voice or text and receive feedback on the specific nature of the gap.
